# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Volunteer rec/youth volleyball coaches — not competitive club coaches, not parents watching, not refs. These are people who signed up to coach a team of 10-year-olds and discovered the hard way that volleyball substitutions are locked to rotation order and position, not a simple clock. They're on their phone in a loud gym. They have to legally rotate every kid in and want the fairness logic handled for them.

Job to be done: get the right kids on the court in the right order, every game, without doing the math in their head under pressure.

## Product Purpose

VolleyCoach is a lineup, substitution, and roster management app for volunteer coaches in rec and youth volleyball leagues. The validated wedge — what makes this different from every existing tool:

- Volleyball-specific: unlike SubTime, CoachMeister, SubNow (all generic "equal time" apps built for soccer/basketball — none support volleyball's rotation-locked sub rules)
- Rec/youth fairness focus: unlike Rotate123 / SoloStats (built for competitive club teams optimizing to win)
- Skill-rating-driven lineup engine: unlike Volleyball Lineup Tracker / Volleyball Rotations (narrow utilities, no fairness intelligence)

Nobody else combines volleyball + mandatory rec-league sub rules + skill-rating-driven lineup suggestions. This is for the coach who has to get every kid on the court, not the coach trying to win state.

Free/trial web version (no $14.99 download required). Primary CTA: try it free, in the browser, right now.

## Brand Personality

Practical, warm, clear.

Sounds like a fellow rec-league volunteer who figured this out and wants to help. "We've been there. Here's what helps." Not a startup. Not a sports analytics platform. Not a hustle-culture productivity tool. A tool a real person built to solve their own problem.

## Anti-references

- **SoloStats / Rotate123**: stats-heavy, data-table-dense, built-for-coaches-who-win aesthetic. Dark sidebars, terminology that assumes coaching background. Wrong audience, wrong energy.
- **Generic sports app**: stock photos of athletes diving, gradient jersey textures, aggressive typography, ESPN energy. This is rec volleyball — kids in mismatched knee pads, not Olympians.
- **Startup SaaS cliché**: beige/cream near-white backgrounds, hero metric cards ("10,000 coaches · 50,000 games tracked"), glassmorphism, identical icon grids, numbered section eyebrows (01 · Features / 02 · Pricing). Looks AI-generated, reads as forgettable.

## Design Principles

1. **The volunteer is the hero, not the software** — copy centers their experience ("you handled it") not feature inventory ("we support 6 substitution types").
2. **Say the wedge out loud** — the rec/youth + fair subs + skill-rating combination is the actual story; it must be explicit and early, not buried in a feature list. Visitors should understand within 10 seconds why this is different from anything they've already tried.
3. **Clarity over cleverness** — every volunteer coach, regardless of tech comfort, should understand what this does and how to start in under 30 seconds. No jargon, no onboarding tax.
4. **Warmth from voice, not decoration** — warmth lives in the copy and tone, not in warm-neutral color backgrounds or rounded-corner softness. A rec-league coach doesn't want a cozy design; they want something that actually works and feels like it was made by someone who gets it.
5. **Mobile-first sideline reality** — this site will be discovered on a phone in a gym or shared in a group chat. Touch targets, legibility, and load speed are non-negotiable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum
- High contrast required — coaches are reading on phones in bright gyms or dim facilities
- Generous touch targets throughout (≥44px)
- Reduced motion support for all animations
- Copy must work without visual context (screen reader order matters)
